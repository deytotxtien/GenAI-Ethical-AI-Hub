☢ Frequently asked questions ☢

1. What is Generative AI?

Generative AI is a type of technology that can create things — such as text, images, music, videos, and even code — based on patterns it learned from huge amounts of data. Instead of just giving information, it can produce new content that didn’t exist before.

2. How does Generative AI work?

It works by studying massive amounts of examples and learning how language, images, or sounds generally look or behave. Once it understands these patterns, it can generate its own versions. Think of it like someone learning by reading thousands of books — eventually, they’ll be able to write in a similar style.

3. What are examples of Generative AI tools?

  • ☣ Chat-based tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini)
    ☣ Image generators (DALL·E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion)
    ☣ Video editors with AI features (Runway ML, CapCut AI tools)
    ☣ Music and voice tools (Suno, AI voice generators)
    ☣ Coding assistants (GitHub Copilot)

    • 4. What can Generative AI do? (Strengths)

  • ☣ Write essays, summaries, and explanations
    ☣Create graphics, art, and logos
    ☣ Help with brainstorming ideas
    ☣ Assist in programming
    ☣ Improve productivity by automating tasks
    ☣ Translate languages and simplify complex topics

    • 5. What are its limitations or risks?

  • ☣ It can produce wrong or inaccurate information
    ☣ It sometimes makes things up (hallucination)
    ☣ AI can sound confident even when incorrect
    ☣ It may reflect biases from the data it learned
    ☣ It can be misused for plagiarism or cheating
    ☣ Privacy risks if people include personal info in prompts

    • 6. Additional FAQs

    ☢ Is GenAI meant to replace humans? ☢

    No. It’s meant to assist people, not replace skills, creativity, or decision-making. Humans are still responsible for accuracy, ethics, and final judgment.

    ☢ Should I rely on AI for everything? ☢

    No. AI is a tool — not a teacher, expert, or authority. It’s great for ideas and drafts, but humans still need to verify, edit, and think critically.
